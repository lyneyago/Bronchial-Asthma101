SYMPTOMS

Understanding the symptoms of bronchial asthma is crucial for effective management. Recognizing and addressing these symptoms early on can help individuals lead a better quality of life. Here, we explore common symptoms associated with bronchial asthma. If you or someone you know is experiencing the following symptoms (Mayo Clinic, 2022), it's crucial to consider the possibility of bronchial asthma:

Wheezing

Wheezing is one of the most evident indicator of Bronchial Asthma. Described as awhistling or squeaky sound when breathing.

Shortness of Breath

Also a symptom widely associated with Bronchial Asthma and othet respiratory conditions. Shortness of bnreath characterizes having difficulties in catching your breath, especially during or after physical activities.

Chest Tightness

This symptom manifests itself as a sensation of pressure (tightness) in the chest often providing a suffocating sensation to the affected individual

Increased Respiratory Rate

Rapid or irregular breathing patterns.

Persistent Cough

Chronic cough associated with the condition. Is known to worsen at night or early morning.

Fatigue

Feeling tired or exhausted. This is caused by the strain exerted on respiratory muscles.

These symptoms can vary in intensity and frequency. It's crucial to seek medical attention if you experience persistent or worsening respiratory symptoms. Early diagnosis and management are key to controlling bronchial asthma and improving overall quality of life. If you suspect asthma, consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and guidance.

Note: This information is for general awareness purposes and should not replace professional medical advice. Always consult a healthcare provider for personalized guidance based on your specific health condition.
